DIRECT OR REGULAR DOCUMENTARY COLLECTION DEAL

Overview of Documentary Collection Deals

Collection Deals must describe two topics:

  • Who is responsible for sending the collection schedule and documents to the Collecting Bank

  • What types of documents must be sent to the Collecting Bank

Responsibility

Depending on the collection type, either the Remitting Bank or the customer is responsible for sending the collection schedule and documents. In either case, the Remitting Bank generates the collection schedule. 

In a Regular Collection, the customer sends all documents to the Remitting Bank.  The Remitting Bank assumes responsibility for transmitting the collection schedule and documents to the Collecting Bank.

In a Direct Collection, the Remitting Bank sends the collection schedule to the customer via the NetIMEX system.  The customer is responsible for sending the schedule and documents. Upon completion of the transaction application, print the collection letter.  The customer then attaches the other documents and sends them to the Collecting Bank.  The Collecting Bank completes the transaction in accordance with the user's instructions.

Since the Remitting Bank is not used as an intermediary, the process of completing a direct collection is faster than that of a regular collection.  However, direct collections require greater oversight on the part of the user.

It is important for the NetIMEX customer to know that the data required and the methods used to perform the regular or direct collections are identical.

Types of Documents

The type of documents being sent, determines if the collection is a Documentary Collection (D/C) or Clean Collection (C/C). 

Documentary Collections refer to a collection of:

  • Financial documents which are accompanied by commercial documents or

  • Commercial documents that are not accompanied by financial documents.

Clean Collection refers to a collection of:

  • Financial documents which are not accompanied by commercial documents.

Some examples of Financial documents include: bills of exchange, promissory notes, checks, or other similar instruments used for obtaining payment of money.

Some examples of Commercial documents include: invoices, transport documents or any other document which is not financial.

Use of the Direct Collection functions must be done with the permission of the exporter's bank.

Note: In NetIMEX, the Direct and Regular Documentary Collections are identical in their usage. Similarly, the Direct and Regular Clean Collections are identical in their usage.  Therefore, in the following explanations, Direct and Regular are used interchangeably.

To Initiate a Documentary Collection Deal

To establish a Direct or Regular Documentary Collection, click on the Direct Doc Collection from the Export Menu.

In the Export functions, a Main Menu appears on the left side of the page. A summary of deals in progress is displayed on the right side of the page.

Click on the Direct or Regular Documentary Collection Deals link or the Template icon in the Main Menu. The Export Direct or Regular Documentary Collection Deals application form appears.

When you click on the Template Icon  an application form with details partly preset appears.

There are five sections that must be completed in order to establish a new Collection.

  • General Details

  • Settlement Data

  • Participants

  • Documents & Terms

  • Text

Before the deal is added to the Release List, the deal must be signed.

To sign the completed deal, click the Sign icon and enter your user name and password.

General Details

The General Details window includes general information fields required for a Documentary Collection Deal.

To complete the General Details section, enter the relevant fields. Some of these fields are mandatory. They are indicated in bold on the screen and by this icon  in this User Guide.

The General Details section requests the following information:

    Amount – Specifies the value to be paid.

    Currency - Specifies the currency in which the amount is defined

Deal Number - This field contains the Deal Number created by the Bank. Note: When NetIMEX is configured to do so, this field will include the bank deal number as generated by NetIMEX.

    Collection Type - This field describes the terms for releasing the documents. There are several types of collections; for example:

  • Documents Against Acceptance - The documents may be released if the drawee signs a draft or Bill of Exchange in which the drawee promises to pay at a future date.

  • Documents Against Payment (Sight) - The documents are released only if the drawee pays the full amount in cash.

  • Payment By Installments - The documents are released when the drawee agrees to pay by installments.

    Send Document Via – Describes how the documents will be sent to drawee.

    Courier Slip No – The slip number of the courier when sent by courier.

Invoices 

At least one invoice, which is included in the collection, must be entered. Insert the number and amount for each invoice.  The system verifies that the total amount of all the invoices is equal to the amount of the collection.

NetIMEX validates that all the information has been entered correctly and the mandatory fields have been specified before displaying the next form.

Invoice No – This is the Invoice Number, copied from the Invoice.

Amount – The amount as listed in the Net Total from the Invoice

To add a new row to the invoice table, enter the details i.e. Invoice Number and Amount in the input fields at the bottom of the table and click on the Add button. The new row will be added at the end of the table.

To update a row in the invoice table double click on the relevant line. The details of the row will be copied to the input fields at the bottom of the table. Change the input fields as required. Click on the Update button; the row will be updated in the invoice table.

To delete an invoice, select the invoice entry and click on the Delete button.

Settlement Data

The second part of the Documentary Collection application form contains the Settlement Data section. Scroll down from the General Details to access this section.

You may also access the Settlement Data section from anywhere in the application form. Clicking on the Settlement Data heading on the yellow Navigation Toolbar that precedes each section will bring you to this section.

The exporter must instruct the bank how payment is to be made. When the agreed payment has been made, the documents can be released to the importer. 

 

The second part of the Export Documentary Collection Data Application Form contains the Settlement Data information and requests the following information:

  • Payment Details

  • Transportation Details

  • Installment Details

Payment Details

There are three settlement Payment options that specify the way payments by the importer can be made.

Indicate the required payment form by clicking on the appropriate radio button at the beginning of the Payment section

  • Sight - The value of the documents is to be paid immediately on presentation.

  • Time - The value of the documents to be paid on a specified date (Due Date), or after a period of time (Tenor Days) and some condition has been fulfilled (Draft After).

  • Installment - The value of the documents to be paid according to an installment schedule; refer to Installment Instructions for further details.

Due Date (relevant for Time) - A specific date the documents are due for payment.

Tenor Days (relevant for Time) - The number of days after a certain event that payment is due.

Draft After (relevant for Time and Installments) - payment is due after a specified event (and the tenor days have lapsed), for example:

  • After B/L Date: This indicates that the Settlement or Tenor terms for payment begin after the date listed on the Bill of Lading.

  • After Date of Invoice: This indicates that the Settlement or Tenor terms for payment begin after the date listed on the Invoice.

  • After Draft Date: This indicates that the Settlement or Tenor terms for payment begin after the date listed on the Draft.

  • After First Presentation (of documents): This indicates that the Settlement or Tenor terms for payment begin after the documents are first presented.

Forward Contract - if the documents are tied to a forward contract, the contract number is specified here.

Transportation Details

Whatever transportation types and details are used, the relevant details must be entered.

Transportation Type - Specifies the mode of transport used for dispatching the goods.  Transportation methods may include: air, combined transport, overland transport, parcel post, sea, etc.

Transportation Name - The name of the vessel or airline.sportation From and To

Transportation From and To - From and to where the goods were shipped.

Transportation Document Number - The ID Number listed on the Bill of Lading \ Airway Bill \ Transport Document.

Transportation Document Date - The date of the shipping document.

Installment Details

When the Settlement Payment Option that specifies the way payment is made on presentation of all the required documents is by Installment, the Documentary Collection Deals is payable according to an installment schedule. This section describes those options.

The Installment Instructions section appears in the Settlement Data part of the Documentary Collection application form. Scroll down from the Settlement Data to access these fields.

 

The (Payment By) Installments portion of the Settlement Data Form displays the following details:

Amount – The amount of the payment due in the specific installment.

Tenor Days – The number of days after a certain event that payment is due.

Due Date – The date the payment is due.

Check Installments Sum – When this checkbox is checked the system will issue an error if the sum of all installments is not equal to the value of the Collection.

 

To add a new row to the installment payment table, enter the details i.e. Amount and Tenor Days or Due Date in the input fields at the bottom of the table and click on the Add button. The new row will be added at the end of the table.

 

To update a row in the table double click on the relevant line. The details of the row will be copied to the input fields at the bottom of the table. Change the input fields as required. Click on the Update button; the row will be updated in the table.

 

To delete a required installment payment, select the payment entry record and click on the Delete button.

  

Add other installment instructions in the General Instructions section in the Text section.

Deal Participants

The third part of the Documentary Collection Deals application form contains the Deal Participants section. Scroll down from the Installment Instructions section to access this section.

You may also access the Deal Participants section from anywhere in the application form. Clicking on the Participants heading on the yellow Navigation Toolbar that precedes each section will bring you to this section.

The Participants form enables you to define the Drawee and Collecting Bank.

 

The following list describes the fields in the Participants section:

Drawee and Collecting Bank Details - Specifies the Drawee and Collecting Bank name and location details relevant to the D/C. Enter the Drawee or Collecting Bank details in the respective blocks. The user may select a Drawee or Collecting Bank by entering a code number or by filling in the name and location details. If the code is entered in the database, the name and location details appear automatically when the code is keyed in.  If the required Drawee or Collecting Bank code does not exist in the database, leave the code field blank and type the full name and address of the Drawee or Collecting Bank in the fields provided.

Search by Code - The database codes that identify the Drawee or Collecting Bank.

Note: The user can identify a specific Party or Bank by entering any number of characters in the Search by Code field and then moving to another field e.g. Enter B and the details of the first Party/Bank that starts with B will be displayed.

Search by Name - The database names of the Drawee or Collecting Bank.

Name - The Drawee or Collecting Bank branch name.  There may be several branches defined for a beneficiary. To define participant details for a new D/C, enter the full Drawee or Collecting Bank name.

Street - The Street name where the Drawee or Collecting Bank is located.

City - The City where the Drawee or Collecting Bank is located.

Country - The Country where the Drawee or Collecting Bank is located.

Default Bank: When a specific Drawee is entered in the Participants section, the Collecting Bank of the last deal using that Drawee together with the currency of that last deal will automatically appear in the Collecting Bank fields as the Default value.

Note: The initial NetIMEX installation includes a list of all the parties and banks which were included in previous transactions with the bank. In the event that the Drawee and Deal Currency details are identical to previous deals, NetIMEX will automatically select the Bank information from those sources.

Additional Parties

 

The number of Additional Parties sections may differ depending on configuration. Each section enables the user to enter information about an additional Party associated with the transaction.

To define a Party:

1.       Select the Party Type.

2.       The system will display the relevant Codes for the select Party Type

The following fields appear in each Additional Party section:

Additional Party Details - Enter the Party details in the respective blocks. The user may select a Party by entering a code number or by filling in the name and location details. If the code is entered in the database, the name and location details appear automatically when the code is keyed in.  If the required Party code does not exist in the database, leave the code field blank and type the full name and address of the Party in the fields provided.          

Search by Code - The database codes that identify the Party. The user can identify a specific Party by entering any number of characters in the Search by Code field and then moving to another field e.g. Enter B and the details of the first Party that starts with B will be displayed.

Search by Name - The database names of the Party.

Name - The Party branch name.  There may be several branches defined for a beneficiary. To define participant details for a new transaction, enter the full Party name.

Street - The Street name where the Party is located.

City - The City where the Party is located.

Country - The Country where the Party is located.

Documents and Terms

The fourth part of the Documentary Collection Deals application form contains the Documents and Terms section. Scroll down from the Participants section to access this section.

You may also access the Documents and Terms section from anywhere in the application form. Clicking on the Documents & Terms heading on the yellow Navigation Toolbar that precedes each section will bring you to this section.

 

The Documents & Terms section of the Collection form is divided into two parts:

  • The upper part contains the deal’s terms.

  • The lower part contains the deal’s documents. 

Terms

Each term has specific responses in the drop down list next to the term. Select the appropriate response for each term.

Documents

The lower part contains the deal’s documents.  After adding document(s) to the Requested Documents list, the user must specify how many copies of each document is required.

Document Description – A list of documents that are required to be presented by the Beneficiary.

No of Originals – Lists the number of original documents that must be presented with the application for a Collection.

No of Copies - Lists the number of duplicates of documents that must be presented with the application for a Collection.

Selecting Documents

Select the requested document(s) required from the Documents List situated in the right-hand side of the Documents section.  Documents may be selected either individually or by holding down the Shift key and clicking on the right mouse button a continuous series of documents maybe selected.  To select a non-continuous series of documents, hold down the CTRL key and select the documents required with the left mouse button.

The selected document(s) are then imported into the Requested Documents window by clicking on the import button  .

Specifying Copies and Originals

Select the required document by highlighting it and either the number of originals and/or copies required have to be entered.  Click on the   button to confirm selection and quantities.

Texts

The last part of the Export Documentary Collection Deal application form allows the user to enter additional customized information and requirements to the Documentary Collection Deal. Scroll down from the Documents & Terms section to access these fields.

You may also access the Texts section from anywhere in the application form. Clicking on the Texts heading of the yellow Navigation Toolbar that precedes each section will bring you to this section.

The Text section enables you to:

  • Enter free text to specific categories.

  • View and specify account details.

 

 

Additional information can be entered for the following categories:

Interest - Use this window to enter any information regarding interest payments.

Agents Commission - Use this window to enter information about any commissions being paid.

Discount - Use this window to enter any information regarding any discounts.

Pay/ACP with Collection Number  - Use this window to enter any instructions regarding payment or acceptance.

General Instructions - Use this window to enter any general information

Goods Description - Used to enter a description of the goods which will appear in the beneficiary’s invoice.

Additional Documents - Use this window to enter any information regarding other documentation.

Note: The bank may change these Text categories.

Zoom Text Icon   – Click on this icon to enlarge the text box area. You may edit or enter new data in the enlarged text box. Minimize the text box to add the material to the Text topics.

Open Clause Library Icon  - When clicking on this icon a list of defined clauses relevant for the window are displayed. After selecting the required clauses the contents of the clause is copied to the window.

Clause Library
In order to display the clauses for a specific text window click on the Open Clause Library Icon next to the relevant window.

 

On the Clause Library window select the clause/s that should be entered into the text by checking the relevant checkbox and clicking on the Select button. In order to include all the Clauses listed in the Clause Library check the checkbox at the top of the column and click on the Select button.

When returning to the Text Screen the user can view the detailed description of the Clause which will be included in the Text. In order to remove a clause from the Text the user must delete the text of the clause from the relevant window.

Defined Clause Descriptions
An additional option exists to display clauses as defined in the clauses form, which the user can select by selecting the desired checkboxes. This replaces the use of the Clause Library and free text windows.

There are two options for this function, which is established during bank setup:

  • The user can select more than one clause from one text window.

  • The user can select only one clause from one text window.

In a text window which is marked as mandatory, a validity check is performed to ensure that at least one clause is selected.

Loan Request

To send a request to the bank to finance the documents prior to receipt of the funds from the drawee, the user can apply to the bank for a loan.  Click on the Texts link on the top of this section or scroll down the page until you reach the Loan Request portion of the D/C form.

 

The following list describes the fields in the loan request window:

Amount - Specifies the loan amount. Enter loan request amount

Currency - Specifies the loan currency. Enter the currency code for the loan.

Loan Type - Specifies the type of loan being requested. Select the loan type from the drop‑down menu

Value Date - Specifies the date the loan becomes effective.

Period - The length of the loan, in days.

Maturity - Specifies the repayment date of the loan if the period is not defined. Either enter the loan period (in days), or the date of maturity.

Account Details

The customer account details window enables you to instruct the bank as to the manner in which to make payment for the various collection items.

 

Up to three Bank Charges can be defined. If data exists in the Payment Item field a corresponding Account number must be entered. If there is a blank Payment Item field, when relevant, any additional Payment Item and corresponding Account number may be selected.

To specify the customer’s account details, select the appropriate field on the Account Instructions Form. The Bank Charges describes the payment item to be paid, for example, Principal Amount, Charges. The Account Number selected from the drop-down list is from the Customer Profile. Use the Additional Instructions window to add additional payment instructions.

Collection Letter Generation

Depending on the set-up of the NetIMEX system the user signing the transaction will have the option of deciding whether to print the Collection Letter.

Upon signing the Documentary Collection the following screen is displayed:

   

If the user clicks on the Print button the Collection Letter will be printed.

If the user clicks on the Cancel button the Collection Letter will not be printed and the user will return to the previous screen.
