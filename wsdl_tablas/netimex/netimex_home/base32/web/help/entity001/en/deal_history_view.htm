Deal History View

Viewing Original Deal Detail

To view the original details please refer to the previous screen (General Details).

Transaction Name – The transaction deal type and step. Click on the Original link to see the transaction as the user entered it and sent it to the bank. Click on other link in the cell to view the response to this transaction as received from the bank

Drw No. - Drawing Number. The Drawing Number is the same as the Transaction Number. It indicates how many actions have been performed on the deal application process.

Amount - The deal amount.

Currency - The currency in which the L/C amount is expressed.

Status - The type of loan, for example regular, past due, full payment and so on.

Status Date - The date and time the transaction changed to the current status.

Expiry Date – The Expiration date of the contract.

View – Click on this icon to view the transaction deal type and step

Transport Doc No – This reference number is taken from the Transportation documents.

Transport Doc Date – This date is taken from the Transportation documents.

Transport Name – This is the name of the shipping company.

Shipment From – This field indicates from where the shipment was made.

Shipment To – The shipping destination.

Viewing Settlement Data Details

If the deal you selected contains Settlement Data details, they will be displayed on the screen.

To view the Open Payments click on the Settlement Data link.

Issue Date – The date the deal was issued.

Commercial Invoice No – The invoice number is found on the Transportation documents.

Commercial Invoice Date – The date of the deal as written on the Commercial Invoice.

When it appears in the original deal, the Acceptances section also appears in the Settlement portion of the form.

 

Maturity Date – The date the collection draft expires.

Acceptance Balance – The remaining amount of the deal to be paid.

To view an acceptance in greater detail, highlight the acceptance record and click on the Transaction Name.

If there are relevant Finance details, they will appear in the Settlement portion of the search results.

Type – Specifies the type of deal. Deal Types include Back to Back, Transfer, Normal and Standby.

Reference Number – The loan reference number.

Principal Balance – The amount remaining on the deal.

Interest Balance – The amount of interest remaining on the deal.

Effective Date – The date when the payment financial obligation begins.

Status  The type of loan, for example regular, past due, full payment and so on.

Status Date and Time - The date and time the transaction changed to the current status.

The Margin section appears in the Settlement portion of the form.

Viewing Deal Participants

To view Deal Participants, click on the Participants link or scroll down the Request Form. The Participants record appears.

Party Code – The customer’s unique ID number assigned by the bank.

Party Name – The customer name.

Street, City Country – The customer’s address.

Viewing Deal Texts

To view all related texts for a deal, click on the Text link or scroll down the Request Form until the Texts section appears. These texts may include incoming correspondences from the bank’s host and incoming Swift messages.  These are texts created by IMEX and transferred via NetIMEX.  To view a document, click on its Transaction Name link to the document entry.

 

To following list describes the fields in the deal history texts window:

Transaction Name – Describes the type of incoming deal.

Drw No. - Drawing Number. The Drawing Number is the same as the Transaction Number. It indicates how many actions have been performed on the deal application process.

Document Name – A more complete description of the Document.

Arrival Date/Time – Tells when the correspondence was sent to or arrived from the bank.

Text ID – Explains the type of document entered into the form.

Send To - Tthe party to whom the text was sent.

Click on the deal listed in the Transaction Name column. The deal text of that transaction appears.
