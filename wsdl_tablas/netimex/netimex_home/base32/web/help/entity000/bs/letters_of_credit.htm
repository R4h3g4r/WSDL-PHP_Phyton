LETTERS OF CREDIT

Overview of Letters of Credit

A Letter of Credit (L/C) is a contract, issued by a bank to an individual or corporation, by which the bank substitutes its own credit for that of the individual or corporation.  The terms of the L/C include the payment amount and timing of payment.  An L/C may be sent from the importer’s bank directly to the exporter, but for security reasons, the L/C is usually sent to the exporter via the exporter’s bank.  The various parties involved in L/C transactions include:

  • The Importer - referred to as the Applicant since the importer requests (applies to) his/her bank to issue the L/C.

  • The Importer Appointed Bank - referred to as the Issuing Bank since the bank issues (opens) the L/C.

  •  The Exporter Appointed Bank - referred to as the Advising Bank since the bank advises the exporter that the L/C has been received.

  • The Exporter - referred to as the Beneficiary since the exporter benefits from the L/C.

  • When there is no account relationship between the Issuing and Negotiating Bank, a third bank may be authorized by the issuing bank to make payments required by the L/C.  This bank is referred to as the Reimbursing Bank.

Establishing a New Letter of Credit

To establish a New Letter of Credit, click on the Letter of Credit link or the Template icon in the Main Menu. The Import Letter of Credit application form appears. When you click on the Template Icon   part of the details are already predetermined and filled in.

There are five sections that must be completed in order to establish a new L/C.

  • General Details

  • Settlement Data

  • Participants

  • Documents & Terms

  • Text

To sign the completed deal, click the Sign icon and enter your password.

General Details

The first part of the Import Letter of Credit application form contains the General Details section. This section appears when you first open a new Letter of Credit.

You may also access the General Details section from anywhere in the application form. Clicking on the General Details title on any heading of the yellow Navigation Toolbar that precedes each section will bring you to this section.

To complete the General Details section, enter the relevant fields. Some of these fields are mandatory. They are indicated in Bold on the screen and by this icon   in this User Guide.

The General Details section requests the following information:

Customer Reference - A unique deal identification number, decided by the NetIMEX user, for example, the importer purchase order number, the contract reference number.

Ref No Not Required – When this checkbox is checked it indicates that the reference number will automatically be generated. When not checked indicates that the reference number will not be generated and the user must enter the reference number manually.

    LC Amount - The L/C amount.

    Currency - The currency in which the L/C amount is expressed.

Deal Number - This field contains the Deal Number created by the Bank. When NetIMEX is configured to do so, this field will include the bank deal number as generated by NetIMEX.

    LC Sent By - Specifies by which mode of transmission the L/C is to be sent to the advising bank.  Methods may include: airmail, courier, mail, SWIFT, telex etc.

    Doc Received Via - Specifies by which mode the relevant documents are to be sent to the Issuing Bank by the Negotiating Bank.

    L/C Type - Specifies the type of L/C. Deal types include:

  • Normal Import L/C: A Regular Letter of Credit.
  • Back to Back L/C: A secondary Letter of Credit issued to a beneficiary on the strength of a primary credit.

  • Transfer L/C: A Letter of Credit that can be transferred to a secondary beneficiary.

Master L/C, L/C Advised By Another Bank  - If the L/C deal type is Back-To-Back or Transfer, the user must enter an L/C Advising Reference check the L/C Advised By Another Bank checkbox.

Goods Origin - Specifies the country from which the goods described in the L/C originate.

    Amount Terms - Specifies whether the terms of payment must be exact or can vary.  Valid payment terms include:

  • Exactly: The value of the documents must conform exactly to the amount of the L/C.
  • About: Documents presented may vary from the L/C amount by a deviation of plus or minus 10% (UCP 500, Article 39a).
  • Vary Amount/Percentage: Vary the payment terms according to the upper and lower percentages specified in the Varied Amount % fields or the varied amount sum stated in the Varied Amount Sum field.
  • Up To: Permits any value up to and including the amount listed.
  • Maximum: Limits the amount to one value. This is the maximum amount allowed, but the amount must be used in its entirety.
  • Not Exceeding: The value of the documents may not exceed the L/C amount.  However, the value can be up to 5% less than the amount, unless the credit states the quantity in terms of a stated number of packing units or individual items (UCP 500, Article 39b).
Select the amount terms from the drop-down list. If the terms are Varied, enter the Varied Amount Sum, or Varied Amount Percentage. If relevant, enter the Varied Quantity Percentage.

Varied Amount Sum - The amount by which the documents may vary from the L/C amount (when the terms are varied).

Varied Amount % - The percentage variation (±) by which the documents may vary from the L/C amount (in the situation that the terms are varied), for example, +12% or -7%.

Varied Quantity % - A percentage variance in respect of the goods to be shipped (if relevant).

    Expiry Date - Specifies the date the L/C expires  Once the date has passed, no additional documents may be presented by the beneficiary.

    Country – Enter the country in which the L/C expires. It is important to the beneficiary since this will effect the time available to present the required documents for payment.

Place - The city in which the L/C expires. This completes the Goods Origin.

Settlement Data

The second part of the Import Letter of Credit application form contains the Settlement Data section. Scroll down from the General Details to access this section.

You may also access the Settlement Data section from anywhere in the application form. Clicking on Settlement Data title on any heading of the yellow Navigation Toolbar that precedes each section will bring you to this section.

 

The second part of the Import L/C application form contains the Settlement Data information of the L/C and requests the following information:

  • Payment Details
  • Transportation Details
  • Shipment Details
  • Installment Details

Payment Details

There are three settlement Payment options that specify the way payment is made on presentation of all the required documents.

To complete the application, select the payment method by selecting (clicking) one of the Payment options (radio buttons).

Sight - The value of the L/C is payable immediately on presentation of the required documents. If payment is by Sight, the Due Date, Tenor Days and Draft After fields are not required.

Time - The value of the L/C is payable on a specified date (Due Date), or after a period of time (Tenor Days) and some condition has been fulfilled (Draft After). If the payment is by Time, enter the Due Date or enter the number of Tenor Days, followed by selecting a Draft After option from the drop‑down list.

Installment - The value of the L/C is payable according to an installment schedule. If the payment is by Installment, define a payment schedule.

Due Date - A specific date the L/C is due for payment (only relevant for Time).

Tenor Days - The number of days after a certain event that payment is due (relevant for Time).

Draft After - Payment is due after a specified event when the tenor days have lapsed (relevant for Time and Installation). Examples of Choices consist of:

  • After Draft Date: When payment is due after the date written on the draft.
  • After B/L Date: When payment is due after the date written on the Bill of Lading.
  • After Custom Clearance: When payment is due after the goods clear customs.
  • After Goods Pass FDA: When payment is due after the goods have been cleared by the Federal Drugs Administration.
  • After First Presentation (of documents): When payment is due after the documents reach the bank where the funds are available.
  • After Arrival of Goods: When payment is due after the goods arrive at the destination. This depends on the shipping terms.
  • After Date of Invoice: When payment is due after the date written on the Shipping Invoice.
  • On Sight: When payment is due after the goods arrive. The responsible party must see the goods and check all documents first.
  • After Dispatch of Goods: When payment is due after the goods have been sent.

Days for Presentation - Specifies the number of days the documents can be presented for payment following the date of the transportation documents. The default time is 21 days, in accordance with I.C.C. regulations. The Days for Presentation and Latest Shipment Date may not exceed the Expiry Date.

    Available With - Specifies which bank is authorized to negotiate the documents on behalf of the beneficiary.  Authorized banks include:

  • Advising Bank: The Beneficiary’s bank.
  • Issuing Bank: The Applicant’s bank.
  • Reimbursing Bank: An Intermediary bank that guarantees the terms as a go between the Advising bank and the Issuing bank.
  • Any Bank (only if the Settlement By is specified as Negotiation): The Exporter may present the Letter of Credit to any bank, and that bank will buy the title, pay the bearer and arrange reimbursement.

    Settlement By - Specifies how payment is made for the deal, and varies according to the payment method.  This is important to the beneficiary since it determines how the L/C is paid. The following are the available options:

  • Payment: The simplest case.
  • Acceptance: When the bank reads and accepts the documents.
  • Negotiation: A bank can sign over the note to another party so, in effect, any bank can use the note.
  • Deferred Payment: Payment is made at a specified later date.
  • Mixed Payment: When more than one of the above methods are used.

Note: As the precise definitions of these terms are extremely complicated, no attempt is made here to define them.  Refer to UCP 500, Articles 9 and 10 for further details.

    Draft Drawn On - Specifies the drawee of drafts under the L/C (if required).  Valid parties include:

  • Advising Bank: The Beneficiary’s Bank.
  • Issuing Bank: The Applicant’s Bank.
  • Applicant: The person who applies for the L/C.
  • Other: Any Intermediary, or the Beneficiary Bank if it is not the Advising Bank.

Note: There is a strict link between the selected Settlement method, and the Draft Drawn on field.  For example, no value is allowed in this field for Payment or Deferred Payment. Refer to your bank for further details.

Forward Contract - If the L/C is tied to a forward contract, the contract number is specified.

Transportation Details

The Transportation option on the Settlement Data Form includes the following fields:

    Incoterm - An international code defining where responsibility for the goods starts and ends, and which party is responsible for the various charges. Depending on the Incoterm selected specific insurance documents must be included. If the required documents are not included, when clicking on SAVE an error message is displayed indicating that there are missing documents.

Transportation Type - Specifies the mode of transport used to send the goods detailed in the L/C.  Some examples of Transportation methods may include: air, combined transport, overland transport, parcel post, sea.

Shipment Details

The Shipment option on the Settlement Data Form specifies the location from where the goods are sent, and the destination of the goods. 

From – Location from which the goods are to be shipped.

To – Destination of the goods.

Earliest – Specifies the earliest date the goods can be shipped.

Latest - Specifies the latest date the goods must be shipped.

The days for presentation and latest shipment date may not exceed the expiry date.

Installment Instructions

The Installment Instructions section appears in the Settlement Data part of the Import Letter of Credit application form. Scroll down from the Settlement Data to access these fields.

When the settlement Payment Option that specifies the way payment is made on presentation of all the required documents is by Installment, the L/C is payable according to an installment schedule.

The (Payment By) Installments portion of the Settlement Data Form displays the following details:

Amount – The amount of the payment due in the specific installment.

Tenor Days – The number of days after a certain event that payment is due.

Due Date – The date the payment is due.

Check Installments Sum – When this checkbox is checked the system will issue an error if the sum of all installments is not equal to the value of the L/C. 

  

To add a new row to the installment payment table, enter the details i.e. Amount and Tenor Days or Due Date in the input fields at the bottom of the table and click on the Add button. The new row will be added at the end of the table.

  

 To update a row in the table double click on the relevant line. The details of the row will be copied to the input fields at the bottom of the table. Change the input fields as required. Click on the Update button; the row will be updated in the table.

 

 To delete a required installment payment, select the payment entry record and click on the Delete button.

Participants

The third part of the Import Letter of Credit application form contains the Deal Participants section. Scroll down from the Installment Instructions section to access this section.

You may also access the Deal Participants section from anywhere in the application form. Clicking on the Participants title on any heading of the yellow Navigation Toolbar that precedes each section will bring you to this section.

The Participants form enables you to define the Beneficiary and Advising Bank.

Note: The initial NetIMEX installation includes a list of all the Parties and Banks which were included in previous transactions with the Bank.

The following fields appear in the Participants section:

Beneficiary and Advising Bank Details - Specifies the Beneficiary and Advising Bank name and location details relevant to the L/C. Enter the Beneficiary or Advising Bank details in the respective blocks. The user may select a Beneficiary or Advising Bank by entering a code number or by filling in the name and location details. If the code is entered in the database, the name and location details appear automatically when the code is keyed in.  If the required Beneficiary or Advising Bank code does not exist in the database, leave the code field blank and type the full name and address of the Beneficiary or Advising Bank in the fields provided.

Search by Code - The database codes that identify the Beneficiary or Advising Bank. The user can identify a specific Party or Bank by entering any number of characters in the Search by Code field and then moving to another field e.g. Enter B and the details of the first Party/Bank that starts with B will be displayed.

Search by Name - The database names of the Beneficiary or Advising Bank.

Name - The beneficiary or Advising Bank branch name.  There may be several branches defined for a beneficiary. To define participant details for a new L/C, enter the full Beneficiary or Advising Bank name.

Street - The Street name where the Beneficiary or Advising Bank is located.

City - The City where the Beneficiary or Advising Bank is located.

Country - The Country where the Beneficiary or Advising Bank is located.

Default Bank: When a specific Beneficiary is entered in the Participants section, the Advising Bank of the last deal using that Beneficiary together with the currency of that last deal will automatically appear in the Advising Bank fields as the Default value.

Note: The Beneficiary details are always mandatory. If the Advising Bank details are not entered the Advising Bank will be selected by the Issuing Bank.

Additional Parties

The number of Additional Parties sections may differ depending on configuration. Each section enables the user to enter information about an additional Party associated with the transaction.

To define a Party:

  1. Select the Party Type.
  2. The system will display the relevant Codes for the select Party Type

The following fields appear in each Additional Party section:

Additional Party Details - Enter the Party details in the respective blocks. The user may select a Party by entering a code number or by filling in the name and location details. If the code is entered in the database, the name and location details appear automatically when the code is keyed in.  If the required Party code does not exist in the database, leave the code field blank and type the full name and address of the Party in the fields provided.          

Search by Code - The database codes that identify the Party. The user can identify a specific Party by entering any number of characters in the Search by Code field and then moving to another field e.g. Enter B and the details of the first Party that starts with B will be displayed.

Search by Name - The database names of the Party.

Name - The Party branch name.  There may be several branches defined for a beneficiary. To define participant details for a new transaction, enter the full Party name.

Street - The Street name where the Party is located.

City - The City where the Party is located.

Country - The Country where the Party is located.

Documents and Terms

The fourth part of the Import Letter of Credit application form contains the Document & Terms section. Scroll down from the Participants section to access this section.

You may also access the Documents & Terms section from anywhere in the application form. Clicking on the Documents & Terms title on any heading of the yellow Navigation Toolbar that precedes each section will bring you to this section.

The Documents & Terms section of the Import L/C form is divided into two parts:

  • The upper part contains the deal’s terms.
  • The lower part contains the deal’s documents. 

Terms

Each term has specific responses in the drop down list next to the term. Select the appropriate response for each term.

Documents

After adding document(s) to the Requested Documents list, the user must specify how many copies of each document are required.

Document Description – A list of documents that are required to be presented by the Beneficiary.

No of Originals – Lists the number of original documents that must be presented with the application for an L/C.

No of Copies - Lists the number of duplicates of documents that must be presented with the application for an L/C.

Selecting Documents
Select the requested document(s) required from the Documents List situated in the right-hand side of the Documents section.  Documents may be selected either individually or by holding down the Shift key and clicking on the right mouse button a continuous series of documents maybe selected.  To select a non-continuous series of documents, hold down the CTRL key and select the documents required with the left mouse button.

The selected document(s) are then imported into the Requested Documents window by clicking on the import button .

Specifying Copies and Originals
Select the required document by highlighting it and either the number of originals and/or copies required have to be entered. Click on the   button to confirm selection and quantities.

Texts

The Text section which is the last part of the Import Letter of Credit application form allows the user to enter additional customized information and requirements to the Letter of Credit. Scroll down from the Documents & Terms section to access these fields.

You may also access the Texts section from anywhere in the application form. Clicking on the Text title on any heading of the yellow Navigation Toolbar that precedes each section will bring you to this section.

The Text section enables you to:

  • Enter free text to specific categories.
  • View and specify account details.

Additional information can be entered for the following categories and may include other custom-defined categories:

Additional Conditions - Used to enter any additional conditions to the transaction.

Comments - Used to enter additional comments.

Customer Instructions - Used to enter additional customer instructions.

    Goods Description - Used to enter a description of the goods that will appear in the beneficiary’s invoice.  Goods Description is a mandatory window in an L/C application.

Additional Documents - To include additional documents that have not been defined by the bank, enter document details in the free format window.

Zoom Text Icon  - Click on this icon to enlarge the text box area. You may edit or enter new data in the enlarged text box.

Open Clause Library Icon  - When clicking on this icon a list of defined clauses relevant for the window are displayed. After selecting the required clauses the contents of the clause is copied to the window.

Clause Library
In order to display the clauses for a specific text window click on the Open Clause Library Icon next to the relevant window.

On the Clause Library window select the clause/s that should be entered into the text by checking the relevant checkbox and clicking on the Select button. In order to include all the Clauses listed in the Clause Library check the checkbox at the top of the column and click on the Select button.

When returning to the Text Screen the user can view the detailed description of the Clause which will be included in the Text. In order to remove a clause from the Text the user must delete the text of the clause from the relevant window.

Defined Clause Descriptions
An additional option exists to display clauses as defined in the clauses form, which the user can select by selecting the desired checkboxes. This replaces the use of the Clause Library and free text windows.

There are two options for this function, which is established during bank setup:

  • The user can select more than one clause from one text window.

  • The user can select only one clause from one text window.

In a text window which is marked as mandatory, a validity check is performed to ensure that at least one clause is selected.

Account Details

The Customer Account Instructions Window enables you to instruct the bank how to make payment for various L/C items such as the documents, bank commissions and charges. 

Up to three Bank Charges can be defined. If data exists in the Payment Item field a corresponding Account number must be entered. If there is a blank Payment Item field, when relevant, any additional Payment Item and corresponding Account number may be selected.

To specify the customer’s account details, select the appropriate field on the Account Instructions Form. The Bank Charges describes the payment item to be paid, for example, Principal Amount, Charges. The Account Number selected from the drop-down list is from the Customer Profile. Use the Additional Instructions window to add additional payment instructions.

Additional Information

This section includes additional details of the transaction. If a field header is bold it indicates that the field is mandatory and the user must enter the relevant text in the corresponding data entry field. If the field header is not bold it is not mandatory and the user does not have to enter data.
